The growing dynamic nature of business processes (BPs) requires ad-hoc decision making. Often these processes involve participants and data from various organizations. Owing to their dynamic decision making and multi organizational nature, a centralized approach to execute these processes would be impractical in cases such as data privacy. This leads to the distributed approach for their execution. A collection of workflow units (WFUs) realizes a distributed business process (DBP) where each WFU is a distributed system component that communicates over publish/subscribe abstraction and is responsible for the automatic execution of a fragment of data or control-flow of a DBP. This necessitates the need of making distributed data-centric workflow management system (based on WFUs) elastic so that the WFUs could be modified during runtime. Here the modification of a WFU does not refer to the modification of its business logic but the external parameters like the nodes they are running on, or the brokers they are connected to.
This thesis provides a safe protocol for WFU movement in such systems based on publish/subscribe. Here the movement of a WFU means that a WFU can to made to run on a different node or can be made to connect to a different broker during runtime. The implementation shows that the protocol can be easily applied on any content-based pub-sub core. Protocol is implemented and tested on D2WORM which is a webbased editor for workflow management system based on GWT. Experiments show that the protocol scales linearly with the number of WFU movements.
